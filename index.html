Q3
Tell me about a leadership moment through real complexity
說一個你領導穿越真實複雜性的時刻
~85s
🎤 ENGLISH · 講這個

"As I mentioned, I'm a pragmatic idealist. When I joined Junyi, our monthly recurring donations were at NT$1.08 million — already Junyi's historical high. But I noticed we had no Meta ads budget at all.

I went to our CEO and asked for one. The problem — I was a new outside hire. Every single full-time member of my team openly objected. They said it was too risky.

Rather than push, I worked with an external consultant to design a framework. We proposed: above 5x ROAS, I could decide the daily budget on my own. Above a threshold, I'd need CEO approval. Below 5x, the budget automatically comes down. Maximized authority — without losing control.

Once CEO authorized this, even my opponents accepted it. Six months later, monthly donations grew from 1.08 million to 1.96 million, with an 8.7x average ROAS.

What I realized — I'm at my best when I find the critical leverage points in a resource-constrained environment, and amplify the outcomes."

📖 中文對照 · 理解結構

「如前所述，我是一個務實的理想主義者。我剛進均一時，每月定期定額捐款是新台幣 108 萬——已經是均一的歷史新高。但我發現我們完全沒有 Meta 廣告預算。

我去找 CEO 要預算。問題是——我是空降的新主管。我團隊每個正職員工都公開反對。他們說太冒險了。

我沒有硬推，而是和外部顧問合作設計一個框架。我們提案：ROAS 高於 5 倍時，我可以自己決定每日廣告預算。超過某個門檻就需要 CEO 同意。ROAS 低於 5 倍時，預算自動下降。權限最大化——但不會失控。

CEO 授權之後，連反對我的人都接受了。六個月後，月捐款從 108 萬成長到 196 萬，廣告平均 ROAS 是 8.7 倍。

我發現——我在資源有限的環境下，找出關鍵槓桿點放大成果，是我最強的地方。」

✂️ 跟長版差異 砍掉「捐款 500→800 的小實驗」整段（如果 Tomoko 追問「還有什麼其他例子」再講）。三段 ROAS 規則精簡。保留：所有關鍵數字、tiered ROAS 框架、leverage points 反思。
Q4
Tell me about the Dengue experience

~75s
🎤 ENGLISH · 講這個 · 慢、莊重

"Lying in that hospital bed with severe Dengue, facing the possibility of dying, I asked myself one question: if I died today, what would I have left behind for this world?

My first thought — and honestly, my only thought — was the work I had been doing at Junyi. The financial foundation I had helped build. [pause 2 seconds] And I realized — even if my life ended right there, it would have been meaningful.

What didn't come to mind — not fame, not wealth, not status. Not even a little.

That changed what I want to leave behind. Not wealth — Legacy. The positive impact that continues even after I'm gone.

I made a promise on that bed: be better to the people who have been good to me. And I've already started — more generous with my family, more time with my closest friends."

📖 中文對照 · 理解結構

我的第一個念頭——而且老實說，是唯一的念頭——就是我這幾年在均一做的事。我幫助打下的財務基礎。[停頓 2 秒] 然後我意識到——就算我的生命在那裡結束，也是有意義的。

那改變了我想留下什麼。不是財富——是 Legacy（遺產）。我離開後仍持續存在的正面影響。

我在那張病床上發誓：對那些對我好的人更好一點。而我已經開始做了——對家人更大方，花更多時間陪我最親近的朋友。」

✂️ 跟長版差異 拿掉「我看待世界的看法改變」段。保留：唯一念頭、三個排除（fame/wealth/status）、Legacy、家人承諾。停頓不能省。
Q5
Tell me about crossing a cultural / values divide
說一個你跨越文化或價值落差的經驗
~70s
🎤 ENGLISH · 講這個

"My work is constantly about leading across people who don't always speak the same language. And honestly — internal is harder than external.

At Junyi, my department is the only one carrying revenue KPIs. I have no formal authority over other departments. Externally, if a partner's values don't align, I can walk away. Internally, I can't.

So my approach is: build trust with the most open partners first. Translate their work into language corporate funders can fund. Support them when they need to quantify impact. And critically — give them the credit when we win.

Underneath this, I hold one belief: we all came to Junyi for the same vision — every child, no matter their background, has the chance to become a lifelong learner. That's the cultural fluency I practice every week."

📖 中文對照 · 理解結構

「我的工作每天都在面對『不總是說同一種語言』的人。但老實說——對內溝通比對外溝通更困難。

在均一，我的部門是唯一扛營收 KPI 的。我對其他部門沒有正式權力。對外，如果合作對象價值觀不合，我可以走開。對內，我走不開。

所以我的做法是：先和最開放的部門夥伴建立信任。把他們做的事情翻譯成企業出資者願意支持的語言。在他們需要量化影響力時提供行銷協助。而最關鍵的——贏的時候把功勞給他們。

底層我有一個信念：我們在均一工作都是為了同一個願景——讓每個孩子，不管出身，都有機會成為終身學習者。這就是我每週都在實踐的文化流暢度。」

✂️ 跟長版差異 簡化「為什麼內部難」的論證、四步驟濃縮成一段。保留：「Internal is harder than external」核心 insight、四步驟方法論、Junyi 願景引用、mic drop 收尾。

🚫 What NOT to Say · 千萬別講的事

  • Don't say "I want to learn from world-class professors." Minerva doesn't lecture — that line signals you misunderstand the model.
    ❌ 不要說：「我想跟世界級教授學習。」Minerva 不講授——這句話會顯示你誤解了他們的模式。
  • Don't list 10 modules. Pick 2 (Leadership in Complexity + Cultural Fluency) and go deep.
    ❌ 不要列 10 個模組。挑 2 個（複雜性領導力＋文化流暢度）深入講。
  • Don't apologize for your English more than once. Acknowledge once, frame as growth, move on.
    ❌ 不要為自己的英文道歉超過一次。承認一次、框架成成長領域、然後往前走。
  • Don't say "Minerva will change my life." Too soft. Say "Minerva will give me X specific capability I'll deploy by Y date."
    ❌ 不要說：「Minerva 會改變我的人生。」太空泛。應該說：「Minerva 會給我 X 這項具體能力，我會在 Y 日期前用上。」
  • Don't say "I just want the network." True but undersells you. Frame as: "I want a cohort to stress-test my frameworks against."
    ❌ 不要說：「我就是想要人脈。」雖然真實但會貶低你自己。改說：「我想要一群同儕來壓力測試我的框架。」
  • Don't recite numbers without context. "8.7x ROAS" alone is jargon. "8.7x ROAS — which meant we proved the framework before scaling" is a story.
    ❌ 不要乾念數字、沒給脈絡。「8.7 倍 ROAS」單獨講是行話。「8.7 倍 ROAS——這代表我們在規模化前先驗證了框架」才是故事。
  • Don't bash Taiwan's education system — even if asked. Frame challenges as opportunities the program will help you address.
    ❌ 不要批評台灣教育體系——就算被問到也不要。把挑戰描述成這個計畫能幫你解決的機會。
  • Don't say "if I don't get this scholarship, I won't be able to attend." Reframe to: "It's about the signal, not just the cost."
    ❌ 不要說：「如果我沒拿到獎學金就沒辦法參加。」改說：「這不只是錢，更是一個訊號的意義。」
  • Don't make jokes about your English. Self-deprecating humor often doesn't translate well in evaluative interviews.
    ❌ 不要拿自己的英文開玩笑。自嘲式幽默在評估型面試中常常會被誤解。
  • Don't pretend to know things you don't. If asked about something specific you haven't seen, say: "I haven't seen that specifically — could you share more?" Honest > bluffing.
    ❌ 不要假裝你知道不知道的事。如果被問到你沒看過的具體內容，就說：「這個我沒有特別看過——你可以多分享一些嗎？」誠實勝過虛張聲勢。
